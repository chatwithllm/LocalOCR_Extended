How it works

Three steps, zero spreadsheets.

Written for humans who would prefer to be doing literally anything else.

Get it in.

Drag, drop, email, or let LocalOCR watch a folder. Crumpled, faded, blurry — send it anyway.

Let it read.

LocalOCR extracts vendor, date, items, total — with confidence scores. Two seconds, usually less.

Breathe out.

Categorized, searchable, queryable. Where did the money go this month? The dashboard tells you plainly.

Built for shared households

Who bought the oat milk? LocalOCR remembers.

Tag receipts by household member. Split restaurant bills, utilities, or groceries automatically. See who owes whom at month’s end — without a single uncomfortable text.

What you get

A household expense system that earns its keep.

01 · Ingest

Phone photo, email, folder drop.

Grab it however you want. Text-message the house LocalOCR address; forward receipts from Amazon; point it at a scanner’s output folder. It all lands in the same place.

02 · Read

OCR with second opinions.

Three vision models work the problem. If the first can’t crack a faded Walgreens receipt, the second tries. You see only the clean result — plus a confidence score for anything uncertain.

03 · Categorize

Smart buckets, your rules.

Grocery, restaurant, utility, subscription, personal, other. Override whenever you want; LocalOCR learns your household’s conventions. “Whole Foods → grocery” stays “grocery,” even when you put cat food in the basket.

04 · Split

No more spreadsheets of shame.

Tag a receipt to a household member or split it evenly, by share, or by item. The “who-owes-whom” summary settles up in one line at month-end.

05 · Watch

Recurring bills, kept honest.

LocalOCR notices when Netflix quietly goes up $2. When your electric bill is 40% over trend. When you paid Amazon Prime twice in a month. Opinions, served with data.

06 · Own

Your data. Your server. Your rules.

SQLite under the hood. CSV and JSON exports. GitHub repo. Your receipts never leave your box except to the vision API you chose (and even that is optional — local models are on the roadmap).
